Discover Five Breweries Located in Harrisonburg

About: Brothers Craft Brewing is a family-owned brewery in Harrisonburg, Virginia. Originally called Three Brothers Brewing, it was founded by the three Shifflett brothers, who grew up in Harrisonburg and always knew they wanted to run a business together.

Flagships: Hoptimization (an American IPA), The Great Outdoors (A Virginia Pale Ale), The Admiral (a Double IPA) and Lil’ Hellion (a Helles Lager).

Fun Fact: The taproom and brewing facility operates out of a renovated soda bottling plant, giving the taproom an old, rustic, and vintage charm that pairs well with drinking craft beer.

About: Gatesman opened The Friendly Fermenter with the goal of creating an atmosphere where people feel comfortable to try what they like. He attributes the success of the brewery to the support he received from friends when he first began homebrewing in 2013.

Flagships: The most popular beers are a honey double IPA called the Bees Knees, a blonde coffee stout called Brehfuss and an Irish multigrain called the Green Jacket. These beers are staples at The Friendly Fermenter.

Fun Fact: When you walk in the doors of the underground nanobrewery you are entering a small community that owner Shawn Gatesman refers to as his village.

About: Opened on April 10, 2015, Pale Fire Brewing Co. is located in the heart of downtown Harrisonburg. It’s one of five Shenandoah Beerwerks Trail stops in the Friendly City.

Flagships: Referred to as its “claim to fame,” Pale Fire’s most popular beer is the Red Molly Irish Red.

Fun Fact: The brewery is named after the novel “Pale Fire” by Russian author Vladimir Nabokov.

About: Restless Moons Brewing, located in Downtown Harrisonburg on Wolfe Street, makes beer for brewery lovers who are looking to try something new. When Moon came across a listing for a brewery called Wolfe Street Brewing, he was living in Washington D.C. and had an interest in homebrewing. Within a few months, Moon and his wife purchased Wolfe Street Brewing, and Restless Moons Brewing was born.

Flagships: Their flagship beer is a pilsner called the Harrisonburger.

Fun Fact: From pilsners to IPA’s to sours, Restless Moons brews small-batch and changes the recipe every time.

About: Originally opened in Charlottesville in 2013, Three Notch’d left its mark in Harrisonburg one year later in 2014 when it opened its second location just blocks away from City Hall. It’s been a staple of the community’s growing downtown ever since.

Flagships: 40 Mile IPA, Minute Man IPA, Ghost APA, Hydraulion Irish Red, King of Clouds Juicy IPA, No Veto Brown, Oats McGoats Oatmeal Stout, Jack's Java Espresso Stout, Local Lager, Blood Orange Gose, and Watermelon Gose are flagships at 3 Notch'd Valley Collab House.

Fun Fact: They are the first satellite brewery in the state.
